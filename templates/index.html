    Phishing Prevention Guide

    Learn how to identify and protect yourself from phishing attacks

    Verify URLs Before Clicking

    Always hover over links to see the actual URL. Phishing links often use misspelled domains like paypa1.com instead of paypal.com. Use PhishGuard's URL Scanner to check any suspicious link before visiting.

    🛡️ Prevention: Use URL Scanner tab to verify

    Analyze Suspicious Emails

    Phishing emails use urgency ("act now!"), threats ("account suspended"), and fake rewards ("you won!") to trick you. Paste suspicious emails into PhishGuard's Email Analyzer to get an objective risk score.

    🛡️ Prevention: Use Email Analyzer tab to scan

    Check SSL Certificates

    A padlock icon alone does NOT mean a site is safe. Free SSL certificates are available to anyone — including phishers. PhishGuard verifies the certificate issuer and expiration to detect fakes.

    🛡️ Prevention: SSL details shown in URL Scanner results

    Expand Shortened URLs

    Never click shortened links (bit.ly, tinyurl) without checking where they lead. Attackers hide malicious sites behind shorteners. PhishGuard automatically expands and analyzes the real destination.

    🛡️ Prevention: Auto-expanded in URL Scanner

    Check Domain Age

    70% of phishing websites are registered less than 30 days before the attack. A brand-new domain claiming to be your bank is almost certainly a scam. PhishGuard checks domain age via real-time WHOIS lookups.

    🛡️ Prevention: Domain age shown in URL Scanner results

    Enable Two-Factor Authentication

    Even if your password is stolen through phishing, 2FA adds an extra barrier. Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Authy) instead of SMS-based 2FA for stronger protection.

    🛡️ Prevention: Enable 2FA on all important accounts

    Never Share Credentials via Email

    Legitimate organizations will never ask for your password, credit card, or SSN via email. If an email asks you to "verify your account" by entering credentials, it's phishing.

    🛡️ Prevention: Email Analyzer detects credential requests

    Report Phishing Attempts

    Report suspicious emails and URLs to your IT department, email provider (mark as phishing), and organizations like PhishTank. Reporting helps protect others.

    🛡️ Prevention: Export PDF reports for documentation

    Audit URLs in Bulk (Organizations)

    IT teams should regularly scan URLs from employee reports, partner communications, and marketing campaigns. PhishGuard's Bulk Scanner processes up to 20 URLs at once with exportable reports.

    🛡️ Prevention: Use Bulk Scan tab for audits

    Stay Educated & Updated

    Phishing tactics evolve constantly. Stay informed about new attack methods through security blogs, training programs, and tools like PhishGuard. Awareness is the strongest defense.

    🛡️ Prevention: Bookmark this guide for reference
